How to organize your library

A well-organized library helps customers find what they need quickly. Here's how categories, tags, and custom filters work together:

Parent Categories

These become the main navigation tabs at the top of your library. Think of them as your top-level sections ‚Äî Datasheets, Manuals, Certifications, Catalogs.

üí° Keep to 5‚Äì8 max ‚Äî too many tabs overwhelm customers

Sub-categories

These appear as sidebar filters when browsing a parent category. Under "Manuals" you might have Installation Guides, User Manuals, and Quick Start.

üí° Use for document types, not brands or product lines

Tags

Labels that work across your entire library, not just one category. Customers can also filter by tags, making it easy to find resources by brand, product series, certification, or language.

üí° A single resource can have many tags ‚Äî great for cross-cutting attributes

Custom Filters

Create up to 5 filter groups for structured attributes that don't fit as categories or tags. Customers see these as additional sidebar filter sections.

üí° Great for Technique, Column Type, Country, or any attribute-based filtering
